Research Experience

  • 2026 - present

    New Haven, CT

    Neurobiological Markers of Opioid Use Disorder
    Postgraduate Associate · Yale University, Dr Sarah Yip
    • Analysed whole-brain FC in OUD (n=53) vs HC (n=38) across MID and Stroop tasks using NBS (268-node Shen atlas); robustness established via NBR and conjunction analysis.
    • Identified large-scale FC networks differentiating OUD from HC; differences persisted in resting-state FC, supporting a stable trait interpretation.
    • Diagnostic FC networks did not predict treatment response, suggesting dissociation between neural markers of OUD pathology and recovery.
  • 2025 - present

    New Haven, CT

    Whole-Brain Resting-State fMRI Prediction of Ambiguity Tolerance
    Postgraduate Associate · Yale University & Rutgers University, Dr Sarah Yip & Dr Anna Konova
    • Applied CPM to resting-state fMRI (n=93) to predict individual differences in ambiguity tolerance from an economic decision-making task.
    • Results suggest ambiguity tolerance requires task-based rather than resting-state connectivity paradigms.
  • 2024 - 2025

    London, UK

    Computational Markers and Clinical Vulnerability in Early Opioid Use Disorder Treatment
    MRes Dissertation (Distinction) · UCL / Yale University
    • Fitted a utility-based computational model to a Risk and Ambiguity Decision-Making Task (n=15, 8-week dense-sampling) to extract ambiguity and risk tolerance parameters.
    • Identified systematic non-random missingness (34.5% excluded); studied as a clinical signal and corrected via propensity score weighting.
    • Mediation analysis revealed distinct dropout pathways: cognitive ability and positive affect were protective; craving, sleep disturbance, and distress intolerance operated through active substance use.
  • 2024 - present

    London, UK

    Mood Velocity and Dynamics in Bipolar Disorder
    Voluntary Research Assistant · UCL, Dr Liam Mason
    • Analysed dense EMA data (N=94, 5×/day, up to 10 weeks) on mood dynamics in bipolar disorder vs healthy controls.
    • Applied Damped Linear Oscillator (DLO) modelling and GLLA to characterise affect lability and dampening parameters.
    • Co-authoring manuscript on NA dampening as a prospective index of manic symptom intensification.
  • 2024 - 2025

    London, UK

    Cross-Country Analysis of Perceived Coercion During COVID-19
    Voluntary Research Assistant · UCL, Dr Veronica Ranieri & Dr Rob Saunders
    • Led multilevel factor modelling and meta-analysis examining perceived coercion across 11 countries; investigated links with anxiety, depression, and emotion regulation.
    • Identified significant cross-country variation in coercion scores, consistently associated with elevated depression, anxiety, and stress symptoms.
